Overview


Coronavirus

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. Most people who fall sick with COVID-19 experiences mild to moderate symptoms and recover without special treatment. The virus that causes COVID-19 is mainly transmitted through droplets generated when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or exhales. These droplets are too heavy to hang in the air, and quickly fall on floors or surfaces. One can be infected by breathing in the virus if he/she is within close proximity of someone who has COVID-19, or by touching a contaminated surface and then your eyes, nose or mouth.

COVID-19 affects different people in different ways. Most infected people develops mild to moderate illness and recover without hospitalization.

Most common symptoms:

  • Fever
  • Dry cough
  • Tiredness

Less common symptoms:

  • Aches and pains
  • Sore throat
  • Diarrhoea
  • Conjunctivitis
  • Headache
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Rash on skin, discolouration of fingers or toes

Preventive Measures for COVID


Protect yourself and others around you by knowing the facts and taking appropriate precautions.
Follow advice provided by your local health authority.




Wash hands frequently

Clean your hands with an alcohol-based sanitiser or soap, especially before preparing food, before eating, or when you get home.



Cough and sneeze into elbow

Coughing or sneezing into your elbow catches the droplets and stops them getting onto hands. It keeps the virus off your hands and prevent the spread



Dispose used tissues immediately

Throw used tissues into the trash, preferably into a container with a garbage bag that you can close tightly and dispose of regularly.



Avoid contact with others

Avoid coming into close contact with others — especially those who are older, sick, or have symptoms of the virus.



Avoid crowd and public gatherings

Avoid frequently going to public places or crowd like markets, malls and parties or functions where social distancing is not properly followed.



Avoid touching your face

Avoid touching eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands, as doing so may transfer virus to the eyes, nose, or mouth and enter the body.



Clean all shared surfaces with sanitiser

Avoid sharing objects and touching surfaces in public spaces. If done so, clean it using a surface disinfectant followed by washing your hands.



Avoid all non-essential travel

People at high risk of COVID-19 complications should avoid cruise and air travel. If not essential or urgent, try to postpone or cancel travel plans.



Call ahead before going to hospital

Calling ahead before visiting a healthcare facility allows healthcare providers to reduce the risk of the virus spreading to others.



Isolate yourself if you are sick

Self-isolate by staying at home and avoiding contact with others, if developed mild symptoms. Stay inside and take rest and prper medication.



Work from home if possible

Encourage work from home options, if feasible for your business.



Wear mask to cover face properly

Make sure your mask covers both your nose, mouth and chin. Don’t use masks with valves and avoid touching its outer surface.
